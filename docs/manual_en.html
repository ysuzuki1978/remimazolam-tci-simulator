1. Overview

Remimazolam TCI TIVA is a pharmacokinetic simulation tool designed for educational and research use. It predicts plasma concentration (Cp) and effect-site concentration (Ce) of remimazolam based on the three-compartment pharmacokinetic model published by Masui et al. (2022) in the Journal of Anesthesia.

The application follows a seamless three-step workflow:

  1. Induction -- Real-time prediction of plasma and effect-site concentrations during anesthesia induction. The user records the moment of loss of consciousness (LOC) to capture the LOC Ce value.
  2. Protocol -- Automated step-down protocol optimization that calculates an optimal continuous infusion rate to maintain a target effect-site concentration derived from the LOC Ce plus a safety margin.
  3. Monitoring -- Entry of actual administered doses (bolus and continuous rate changes) with simulation of the resulting concentration-time curves. Supports CSV data export.

Data flows automatically between steps: the LOC Ce recorded in Step 1 transfers to Step 2, and the optimized protocol from Step 2 pre-populates dose events in Step 3.

Not for Clinical Use This application is strictly for educational and research purposes. It must not be used as a basis for clinical diagnosis or treatment decisions. All clinical decisions must be made under the responsibility of qualified medical professionals. Calculation results are theoretical values.

2. Getting Started

2.1 Installation (PWA)

The application is a Progressive Web App (PWA). Once loaded in a browser, it can be installed to your home screen and used offline. This is particularly useful for iPhone and iPad users in environments without reliable network access.

To install on iPhone / iPad (Safari):

  1. Open the application URL in Safari.
  2. Tap the Share button (square with upward arrow) in the browser toolbar.
  3. Scroll down and tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Add to confirm. The app icon will appear on your home screen.

After installation, the app launches in full-screen mode without browser navigation bars and works entirely offline.

2.2 Disclaimer Screen

When the application starts, a disclaimer modal is displayed. You must read and accept the terms before proceeding.

Figure 1: Disclaimer / Important Notice modal

Tap Accept and Start to proceed to the main application.

2.3 Patient Settings

Patient parameters directly affect pharmacokinetic calculations. To configure patient information, tap the patient summary button (showing age and weight, e.g., "50y 70kg") in the upper-right corner of the header.

Figure 2: Patient Information modal

3. Step 1: Induction -- Real-time Prediction

Step 1 simulates real-time pharmacokinetic prediction during anesthesia induction. It models the concentration-time profile after a bolus injection with concurrent continuous infusion, updating the display every second.

Figure 3: Step 1 initial state

3.1 Setting Bolus and Continuous Rate

Before starting the real-time simulation, set the initial dosing parameters:

Parameter Unit Range Default Step
Bolus mg 1 -- 15 7 0.5
Continuous mg/kg/hr 0 -- 6 1.0 0.1

Use the - and + stepper buttons to adjust values, or tap the number field to enter a value directly.

3.2 Running the Prediction

  1. Tap Start to begin the real-time simulation. The timer starts and concentration values update every second.
  2. Observe the three concentration display cards: Plasma Cp (left), Effect-site Ce (center, highlighted in cyan), and Elapsed time (right).
  3. The real-time chart plots both Cp (blue line) and Ce (green line) against elapsed time.
  4. Use the Record button to save a snapshot of the current concentrations at any point.
  5. Tap Stop to pause the simulation.

Figure 4: Step 1 running with real-time concentration chart

3.3 Recording LOC (Loss of Consciousness)

The LOC Ce value is a critical measurement that defines the effect-site concentration at the moment the patient loses consciousness. This value is used in Step 2 to calculate the target maintenance concentration.

  1. While the simulation is running, observe the patient for signs of loss of consciousness.
  2. At the moment of LOC, tap the LOC button. The current Ce value is recorded as the LOC Ce.
  3. The LOC Ce value appears in a prominent yellow-green display card labeled "LOC Ce (Sleep Onset)".
  4. A record entry is also saved automatically in the Records section below the chart.

Figure 5: Step 1 after LOC Ce recorded (0.123 microg/mL in this example)

After recording the LOC Ce value, tap Proceed to Protocol at the bottom of the screen to advance to Step 2. The LOC Ce value transfers automatically.

4. Step 2: Protocol -- Step-down Optimization

Step 2 calculates an optimized step-down infusion protocol. Starting from the initial bolus and continuous rate, the algorithm progressively reduces the infusion rate over time while maintaining the effect-site concentration at or above the target level.

Figure 6: Step 2 with LOC Ce transferred from Induction

4.1 Safety Margin and Target Ce

If a LOC Ce was recorded in Step 1, a cyan banner at the top of Step 2 displays the transferred value (e.g., "LOC Ce from Induction: 0.123 microg/mL").

The target effect-site concentration is calculated as:

Target Ce = LOC Ce + Safety Margin

The safety margin is adjustable in increments of 0.01 microg/mL (default: 0.15 microg/mL). For example, with a LOC Ce of 0.123 and a margin of 0.15, the Target Ce becomes 0.273 microg/mL.

4.2 Protocol Parameters

Parameter Unit Default Description
Target Ce microg/mL 0.40 (or calculated from LOC Ce) Desired steady-state effect-site concentration
Bolus mg 7 Initial bolus dose for the protocol

4.3 Advanced Settings

Tap Advanced Settings to expand additional optimization parameters:

Reduction Factor 70% 50 -- 90% Percentage to reduce the infusion rate by at each step-down
Adj. Interval 5 min 3 -- 10 min Minimum time between rate adjustments

4.4 Optimization Results

Tap Execute Optimization to run the algorithm. The results section displays six metrics:

  • Optimal Rate -- The final steady-state continuous infusion rate (mg/kg/hr)
  • Final Ce -- The predicted effect-site concentration at the end of the simulation window (microg/mL)
  • Accuracy -- Percentage deviation from the target Ce
  • Adjustments -- Number of step-down rate changes performed
  • Stability -- Stability index (out of 100)
  • Convergence -- Time in minutes for the Ce to converge to the target range

Figure 7: Step 2 optimization results

Figure 8: Step 2 protocol concentration chart

Below the chart, expand Dosing Schedule to view a detailed table of all rate changes with their timestamps and infusion rates.

Tap Monitoring at the bottom-right to transfer the optimized protocol to Step 3. The protocol data (bolus dose and step-down schedule) is automatically loaded as dose events.

5. Step 3: Monitoring -- Actual Dose Tracking

Step 3 allows you to enter the actual doses administered during a procedure and simulate the resulting concentration-time profiles. If protocol data was transferred from Step 2, the dose events are pre-populated.

Figure 9: Step 3 with protocol data transferred from Step 2

5.1 Adding Dose Events

Each dose event represents a point in time when the dosing changed. An event consists of a timestamp, an optional bolus amount, and/or a continuous infusion rate.

  1. Tap + Add in the Dose Events section header.
  2. In the modal, set the Time (HH:MM format, relative to the anesthesia start time shown below the field).
  3. Enter a Bolus amount in mg (set to 0 if no bolus is given at this time point).
  4. Enter the Continuous infusion rate in mg/kg/hr.
  5. Tap Add to save the dose event.

Pre-populated events from Step 2 appear in the event list with their time offsets (e.g., "0 min (08:00)", "15 min (08:15)"). Each event shows its bolus and continuous rate, with a red delete button on the right to remove individual entries.

5.2 Running the Simulation

  1. After entering or confirming all dose events, tap Run Simulation.
  2. The results section displays three summary cards: Max Cp, Max Ce, and Method (the calculation engine used).
  3. The concentration chart plots the full Cp and Ce time curves based on the entered dose events.

Figure 10: Step 3 simulation results with concentration summary

Figure 11: Step 3 monitoring concentration chart

5.3 CSV Export

Tap Export CSV to download the simulation data as a CSV file. The export includes timestamped concentration values (Cp and Ce) along with the dose event data, suitable for further analysis in spreadsheet software or statistical tools.

Tip On iOS, the CSV file can be saved to the Files app or shared directly via AirDrop, email, or other share sheet options.

7. Tips and Best Practices

Workflow Sequence

The three-step workflow is designed to be followed in order. While you can navigate between steps using the step tabs at the top, the intended flow is: Induction, then Protocol, then Monitoring. Data transfers only in the forward direction.

Patient Parameters

  • Always set patient parameters before starting any simulation. The PK model calculations depend on age, weight, height, sex, and ASA-PS.
  • BMI is calculated automatically from weight and height and displayed in the patient modal.
  • The patient summary in the header (e.g., "50y 70kg") provides a quick confirmation of the active patient profile.

LOC Ce Recording

  • Tap the LOC button at the exact moment of observed loss of consciousness for the most accurate Ce value.
  • The LOC Ce is the foundation for Step 2 target concentration calculation. An accurate LOC reading improves protocol optimization.
  • If you need to re-record the LOC, stop the simulation, reset, and start again.

Safety Margin Selection

  • The default safety margin of 0.15 microg/mL adds a buffer above the LOC Ce to reduce the risk of awareness.
  • Adjust the margin based on clinical context: higher margins provide a greater safety buffer; lower margins reduce total drug exposure.

Offline Use

  • After installing as a PWA, the app works fully offline. No network connection is needed during use.
  • CSV export saves files locally to the device.

Screen Orientation

The app is optimized for portrait orientation on iPhone. Charts and controls are designed for single-hand operation on mobile devices.

8. Troubleshooting

The app does not load or shows a blank screen

Ensure you are using a supported browser (Safari on iOS, Chrome or Edge on desktop). Clear the browser cache and reload the page. If using the PWA, try removing and reinstalling the app.

LOC Ce is not transferred to Step 2

Confirm that the LOC button was pressed during Step 1 and the LOC Ce value is displayed in the green card. Navigate to Step 2 using the "Proceed to Protocol" button rather than directly tapping the Step 2 tab.

Protocol data is not transferred to Step 3

Ensure that you have executed the optimization in Step 2 and results are displayed before navigating to Step 3. Use the "Monitoring" button at the bottom of Step 2 for the transfer.

Concentration values seem incorrect

Verify that patient parameters (especially weight) are correctly set. The PK model is sensitive to patient demographics. Also confirm the units: bolus is in mg (total dose), while continuous rate is in mg/kg/hr (weight-based).

CSV export does not work

On iOS, the file download behavior may differ depending on the browser. In Safari, the file may open in a new tab or prompt a save dialog. Try using the Share sheet to save to the Files app.

Diagnostics button

The gear icon in the upper-right corner of the header opens an error diagnostics panel. This is primarily for development and debugging purposes. If you encounter unexpected behavior, the diagnostics panel may provide error details useful for reporting issues.
